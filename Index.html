Johnny Cash

Here's a timeline of Johnny Cash

  • Feb. 26, 1932-He is born in Kingsland, Arkansas.
  • May 1944-Johnny's brother is almost cut in half by a table saw and dies later from the injuries.
  • 1950-Johnny's drug and alcohol addictions and abuse begins.
  • 1950-This is when J.R. Cash becomes Johnny Cash. The army wouldn't allow a name of initials.
  • August 1954-Johnny Cash meets Vivian Liberto and they later marry after he is honorably released from the army.
  • 1955-Johnny Cash records his first sinlge with Sun Records, "Hey Porter."
  • May 24, 1955-The birth of Johnny's daughter, Rosanne.
  • 1957-"Walk the Line" becomes No. 1 on country charts.
  • 1965-Johnny is arrested in El Paso, Texas for possesion of narcotics.
  • 1966-Vivian files for a divorce because of Johnny's drug abuse and multiple affairs, one being the close relations with June.
  • 1966-Johnny is arrested in Starville, Mississippi for trespassing on private property. He had been picking flowers.
  • 1967-Johnny was arrested and then released after a long talk with a sherrif about his wasted potential. He credits this talk to "saving his life," and then does a benefit concert that raises $75,000 for a local high school.
  • 1967-June and Johnny accept their Grammy for Best Country and Western Performance, Duet, Trio or Group for their song "Jackson."
  • Oct. 1967-Johnny attempts suicide by crawling into Nickajack Cave in Tennessee. He was heavily influenced by drugs. He then had a spiritual revelation, and felt like God saved him.
  • March 1, 1968-After proposing on stage, Johnny and June get married.
  • March 3, 1970-Johnny's only son is born.
